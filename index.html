AI and the monorepo question

Something I've noticed while working with AI-assisted development: AI seems to work better with monorepos.

As humans, modular multi-repo setups feel cleaner. Separate services, clear ownership, obvious boundaries — easier to reason about what belongs where.

But for AI, context is everything. In a monorepo, the model sees shared types, packages, and helpers in one place, making it far easier to understand how a change in one part of the system ripples into another. With multiple repos, you can still feed the AI what it needs, but cross-repo dependencies are easy to miss unless you're deliberate about surfacing them.

The tradeoff feels different now:
Multi-repo → cleaner for humans.
Monorepo → easier for AI.

Whether that matters depends on your team size, architecture discipline, and how much you're leaning on AI tooling day-to-day. But if your answer to the last two is "a lot" — it might be worth reconsidering the default.
